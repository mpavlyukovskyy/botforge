Hi Kristina — a quick update from Mark on how task pay works, now that the board and priorities have settled in. Nothing here is meant to catch you out; the changes are all in your favour, and I wanted you to have the full picture.

How a task earns:
- Every task is worth a base amount. Mark's priority sets a multiplier: Routine counts a little less, Standard is the normal rate, and Important / P0 (drop-everything) are worth more. So focusing on the high-priority items genuinely pays off.
- Finish on time and you earn the full amount. If something runs late the value tapers down over time, but it can never go below zero. You can't "lose" money on a task. The worst case is it earns nothing.

When something isn't your fault:
- If you're waiting on someone else (a vendor, a reply, Mark himself), just tell me you're blocked and who you're waiting on. The clock pauses while you wait, so being stuck on a third party never costs you. Tell me when it's unblocked and it picks back up.

Big tasks:
- If something is really a project, Mark or I can break it into milestones. Finishing all the milestones is worth the whole project. It's split across them, not stacked, so it works out the same either way. You just get credit as you go instead of all at the end.

A couple of things only Mark does:
- He can mark a piece of work "excellent" for a small bonus on it.
- If something needs another pass, he can send it back as rework. You don't lose anything. Redoing it simply earns again.

What you do day to day doesn't change at all: work the Top 3, mark things done the way you always have, tell me when you're blocked, and reply to my check-ins. That's it. This just makes the rewards line up with the work. Thank you for everything you do.
